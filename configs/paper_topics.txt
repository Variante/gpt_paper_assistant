1. Application of diffusion models and vision-language models (VLMs) to robotics.
   - Relevant: papers using video diffusion models, learned world models, or VLMs to enable robotic policy learning, task planning, open-vocabulary manipulation, or instruction-following; papers that leverage VLMs for task grounding in robotic control loops; papers applying generative models as robot world models or for data augmentation in robot learning.
   - Not relevant: papers whose primary contribution is improving diffusion model architecture, generation quality, or inference speed without a robotics application; papers that use VLMs for pure perception, image captioning, or QA without any robotic control loop; papers on 3D or video generation as an end in itself.
2. New methodological improvements to self-supervised learning (SSL) for image or video representation.
   - Relevant: papers proposing or rigorously analyzing specific SSL methods such as contrastive learning or masked autoencoders, with a clear focus on representation quality.
   - Not relevant: papers that merely apply a pretrained SSL backbone to a downstream task without modifying the SSL method itself.
3. Video segmentation using unsupervised or self-supervised methods.
   - Relevant: methods for video object or semantic segmentation that avoid manual labels, evaluated on standard benchmarks.
   - Not relevant: papers that apply existing segmentation pipelines to specialized domains (medical, satellite, agricultural) with only minor modifications.
4. Transfer learning across modalities (e.g., audio-to-video, optical flow, language-to-video) for improved video understanding.
   - Relevant: methods that leverage domain-agnostic or cross-modal data to improve dense video representations or temporal reasoning.
   - Not relevant: papers on image classification or coarse action recognition that do not require pixel-level or temporal understanding.
5. Advances in 3D generation using generative models, including image-to-3D and text-to-3D.
   - Relevant: papers that substantially improve generative model quality or efficiency for 3D content creation, or that analyze the fidelity of generated 3D assets.
   - Not relevant: work focused solely on 2D image generation or on non-generative aspects of 3D modeling.
6. Recent progress in 3D reconstruction and generation with Gaussian Splatting, NeRF, or mesh generation.
   - Relevant: papers on 3D reconstruction from sparse or pose-free multi-view images, or methods that jointly estimate camera poses and perform reconstruction.
   - Not relevant: papers that only incrementally improve NeRF on standard benchmarks (NeRF synthetic, LLFF) without introducing a substantially new approach.

 In suggesting papers, remember that your friend is primarily interested in self-supervised learning, computer vision, robotics, video understanding, and cross-modal transfer. He values methodological novelty and clear empirical contributions over domain-specific applications.
