1. Advances in video/image diffusion models and vision-language models (VLMs), including their application to robotics.
   - Relevant: papers improving generation quality, reducing inference or training cost, or analyzing diffusion model fidelity; papers using video diffusion models, learned world models, or VLMs to enable robotic policy learning, task planning, open-vocabulary manipulation, or instruction-following; papers that leverage VLMs for task grounding in robotic control loops.
   - Not relevant: discrete or language diffusion models whose main contribution is a 3D generation application; papers that use VLMs for pure perception or QA without any robotic control loop; papers that only apply language models (without video generation or VLM grounding) to robotics.

 In suggesting papers, remember that your friend is primarily interested in self-supervised learning, computer vision and robotics. He values methodological novelty and clear empirical contributions over domain-specific applications.
